Focaccia Recipe

Description

Focaccia is a flat leavened oven-baked Italian bread, similar in style and texture to pizza; in some places, it is called pizza bianca.

Focaccia can be served as a side dish or as sandwich bread and it can be round, rectangular, or square shape.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 sprig rosemary

Steps

  1. Whisk together the flour, salt, and yeast first:
  2. Add the water:
  3. Use a spatula to stir the two together.
  4. Slick the dough with olive oil:
  5. Slick the surface of the dough with olive oil; then cover the bowl.
    Stick the bowl in the fridge immediately;
    leave it there to rise for 12 to 18 hours (or longer
    —I’ve left it there for as long as three days).
  6. Deflate the dough and transfer to a prepared pan. I love this 9×13-inch USA pan.
    If you don’t have one you can use two 8- or 9-inch pie plates or something similar.
    If you are using glass baking dishes be sure to grease the dishes with butter before pouring a tablespoon of olive oil into each.
    (The butter will ensure the bread doesn’t stick.) Don’t touch the dough again for 2 to 4 hours depending on your environment.
  7. it’s time to dimple it! You can use simply olive oil and salt — I recommend good, flaky sea salt for this.
    Note, the dough in the photo below spent three days in the fridge, and the dough was super bubbly!
  8. if you are using rosemary, sprinkle it over the dough. Then pour two tablespoons of olive oil
    over the dough, and using your fingers,
    press straight down to create deep dimples. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt — again, something like Maldon is great here.
  9. Transfer to oven immediately and bake at 425ºF for 25 minutes or until golden all around. Remove focaccia from
    pans and place on cooling racks.
